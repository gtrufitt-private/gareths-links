Summary

The article advises on effective API design specifically focusing on the structuring of object Identity numbers (IDs), arguing for the use of unique, non-predictable IDs, such as the Universally Unique Identifier, and the addition of a prefix to make IDs human readable. The use of a prefix can also facilitate debugging, prevent human error, and allow different objects and types of information to be easily differentiated and accessed in a database.

The Back-end for Front-end Pattern (BFF)

:speaking_head_in_silhouette: Older article but a good one about how the BFF term was coined at Soundcloud

Summary

Engineers at SoundCloud experimented with different architectural structures to manage service usage and reducing overlap to increase system efficiency, ultimately settling on a pattern they called "Back-end for Front-end" (BFF). Essentially the BFF solution offers better flexibility for developers, enabling them to aggregate information better, ease pressure on the API, and reduce response time in slower, wireless networks.

Death by a thousand microservices

Summary

The software industry is returning to the philosophy that overly complex systems, specifically microservices-based systems, can hinder productivity and growth. Patterns highlighted in a summary of five years of startup audits, such as collective imposter syndrome when building simple, straight-forward systems, reveal that complexity often results in waste and inefficiency, leading to companies failing to solve problems that they do not have.
